"The poem is an expedition in the world": Edouard Glissant and experiments in cartographic mapping

Drawing inspiration from Margaret W. Pearce and Michael J. Herman’s polyvocal narrative map of Champlain’s travels and interventions in post-representational mapping (Kitchin and Dodge 2007; Kitchin et al. 2013; Del Casino and Hanna 2005), I wanted to mapping Edouard Glissant’s poetic writings on landscape and identity. Edouard Glissant is a Martinican philosopher famous for his novels, poetry, and essays which articulate a notion of (Caribbean) postcolonial identity beyond the relationship between the colony and the metropole. In the mappings that follow, I take excerpts and quotes from several of Glissant’s essays and books that outline his philosophy of poetics and Relation: Sun of Consciousness (trans. 2020), Poetic Intention (trans. 2010), Caribbean Discourse(trans. 1989), Poetics of Relation (trans. 1997), and Treatise on the Whole World (trans. 2020). Glissant’s oeuvre not only challenges “the ontological security” of transcendental Western epistemologies and approaches to cartography, but can also offer new ways to read maps as mappings, transitory and fleeting, open to new relations and interpretations (Kitchin and Dodge 2007).

While this map intends to tell the story of Glissant’s theoretical oeuvre and convey a notion of his philosophy, I hope to also interrogate how narrative and poetics in cartography can be used both as a means of representation and performance (Del Casino and Hanna 2005). I particularly hope to convey Glissant’s philosophy of Relation which is archipelagic in expression, and rhizomatic and open to new contingencies, emergences, and becomings. Landscapes, in particular, take on a poetic and lyrical character involved in these processes, challenging and confronting static representational politics.

What I hope to show in these mappings is the falling apart of topography, an opacity that conveys the density of relation in the world, a becoming-indiscernible, that gestures to the diffractive potential of Glissant’s thinking for cartography.
