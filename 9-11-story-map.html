“INS, FBI, No More Kidnaps, No More Lies”: Hate violence and state terror in the wake of 9/11

[content warning: descriptions of prison abuse, audio testimony of police violence]

Anti-Asian American hate violence is back in the spotlight following the 20th anniversary of 9/11. Drawing on archival research from South Asian-American organizing in the post-9/11 period, this story map looks at how the focus on hate violence overlooks the egregious violations of human rights and racist state terror enacted by the United States government in South Asian, Arab, and Muslim communities in the wake of 9/11. The infrastractures of surveillance, policing, and immigrant detention built in the aftermath of 9/11 remain in place today, and continue to harm Black and brown immigrant communities.
