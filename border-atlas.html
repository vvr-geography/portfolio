Whose Past, Whose Present: Monuments and Settler Colonialism

What are the impacts of statues and monuments on our landscapes and how do they contribute to the legacy of settler colonialism? Drawing from indigenous and counter-cartographies, this atlas spread is an attempt to show how discourses of settler colonialism and border imperialism are materialized on landscapes in the U.S. and Canada. Using popular education techniques and examples, I walk readers through the process of how to read the effects of settler colonialism on the landscape and explore the potential for Indigenous resistance and land reclamation.
